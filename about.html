WHO IS DARD?

The Disabled Agency for Rehabilitation and Development (DARD), a National Non-governmental Organization (NNGO) founded in 2003; the organization was founded in order to represents the voice of voiceless through lobby, advocacy and empowerment of the vulnerable members of the communities in South Sudan.


LEGAL STATUS

DARD is legally registered with the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission (RRC) as a National NGO under Chapter 3, section 10 of NGOs Act. 2016 with Reg. Number 1019. We are also registered with -PADOR as member to Europe Aid member.


VISION

A transformed and an inclusive Society where persons with disabilities and other marginalized groups can live their lives in dignified conditions free from poverty, discrimination and violence.


MISSION STATEMENT

We work together for a positive and sustainable change in lives of People with disabilities, children, Youth & women affected by poverty, discrimination, violence and injustice.


MAIN OBJECTIVE
  • To change unjust economic, social and cultural veave that generates poverty, discrimination, injustice and violence within the society.
  • To become self-sustained members who overcome their sufferings and poverty through livelihood activities and evidenced-based-advocacy.

OUR CORE VALUES
  • Human dignity and rights.
  • Equality and equity principles.
  • Quality service delivery.
  • Transparency and accountability.
  • Inclusivity and non-discrimination.

ORGANIZATION'S STRUCTURE
  • Strategic level.
  • Tactical level.
  • Management Committee/Team.

PROGRAMMES

The eight focus areas (8FAs) for DARD’s work are:

  1. Communication and Policy Advocacy & Awareness.
  2. Inclusive Education & Skills Development.
  3. Livelihood, Food Security & Livelihoods, Nutrition, resilience, & Climate Change.
  4. Civic Engagement for Good Governance & Democracy.
  5. Human Rights, Protection and Child Rights Governance.
  6. Humanitarian Services, EP& R and WASH
  7. Peace-building, Community Security & Safety and Conflict resolution
  8. Institutional Development

LOCATIONS/AREAS OF OPERATIONS:

DARD has been operating in many different counties of Lakes State mentioned below: Rumbek centre, Rumbek East, Wulu, Cueibet, Yirol West, Rumbek North counties and currently have a liaison office in Juba looking for possibilities to extend our presence and services to other areas across South Sudan and beyond.


PAST EXPERIENCES/PERFORMANCES

DARD has been operating in Lakes State since 2005 and has supported both short and long-terms activities such as; Awareness and advocacy on disability, Inclusive Education, Peace building & Community Security, WASH, Governance & democracy, Child protection, child rights governance, Humanitarian services and resilience programs at grassroot levels.. Engaged with UNMISS Civil Affairs as IP for QIPs in 2007-2012

We also support International events such as Africa Charter on Rights and Welfare of the Child (ACRWC) day, 3rd December International Day of Persons with Disabilities, Women International Day and the Girl child education including back to learning campaigns with other stakeholders.

DARD implemented a project on governance funded by EC through Oxfam GB, these projects were composed of many components of which Peace building and conflict mitigation is being incorporated as one of the cross-cutting issues implemented by DARD through peace dialogues, workshops with the community stakeholders in Cueibet County for the last three years.

DARD also in partnership with Pact South Sudan has implemented an EPS (Enhancing Peace and Security) project in Lakes state since 2013-2014.

In 2014 up to date, we are implementing a three year governance project in partnership with Oxfam GB funded by EU for Lakes state and also working on GBV in partnership with IRC South Sudan program.

In 2017, DARD implemented a peace-building and conflict resolutions project in partnership with CRS for 6 months in Rumbek East County. The project produce a better result between two sections of Gony and Thuyic resulted to a 4 years sustainable peace between the two parties till they clashed again in 2020.


OUR CURRENT/ACTIVE PROJECTS

DARD has 3 active long term projects funded by different partners in different locations. Two of these projects are supporting education and governance while the other one is a community security and peace project as detailed below:

  1. Saving lives now and in future through education, inclusion and enhancing local capacity in South Sudan.

    2. This project is implemented by DARD in partnership with Oxfam International with funding from DANIDA. It is supporting education, governance and peace components. The project targets 10 schools with capacity building trainings to teachers, PTAs and learners on literacy and numeracy, scheme of work and lesson plan development including school management plans, it also supports community peace dialogues amongst the conflicting communities and provide capacity building trainings to local government authorities on governance, peace and conflict management.

  2. The NORAD Funded project implemented by DARD

    3. The NORAD funded project implemented by DARD in partnership with Save the Children International is a five (5) years project supporting education, child rights governance (CRG) and Child Protection components. It is implemented in 15 different schools in Rumbek East and Rumbek centre. It also supports advocacy activities on the inclusion of persons with disabilities with major engagement on the policy and UNCRPD ratification at Juba level.
